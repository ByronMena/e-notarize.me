What is a Notary Public, and what do they do?

A notary public is a public officer appointed and commissioned by the Governor whose function is to administer oaths, to take acknowledgments of deeds and other instruments, to attest to or certify photocopies of certain documents, and to perform other duties specified by law.

What is Remote Online Notarization (RON)?

Remote Online Notarization (RON) allows individuals to have their documents notarized online, with the notary and signer communicating through audio and visual technology. The signer must go through ID Verification with Biometrics and pass a knowledge-based Authentication (KBA) questionnaire pulled from official state sources.

What do I need to bring for notarization?

Bring a valid government-issued photo ID (such as a driver's license or passport) and the document to be notarized. Do not sign the document beforehand; the notary must witness your signature.



Acceptable Identifications
  • Government-issued driver's license or identification card (Unexpired)
  • U.S. Passport
  • Permanent residence card "green card"
  • Foreign passport stamped by USCIS
  • U.S. Military ID
  • Inmate ID
  • Veteran Health ID
  • Mexico / Canada's Driver's License
